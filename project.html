#Changing data type for date from factor to date

data$date <- as.Date(data$date, format = "%m/%d/%Y")
#head(data)
#Date format is in YYYY-MM-DD

#Changing the data type for age from factor to int
data$part1_age <- as.integer(data$part1_age)
#head(data)
#str(data)

#Next, we drop rows with NA values using na.omit.

data_clean <- na.omit(data)

# Finally, we noticed in our two factor variables, state and city_or_county, had levels "" (empty strings) that had to be removed as they were unwanted.

# We did this using the following code to drop levels.

data_clean$state[data_clean$state == ""] = NA
data_clean$state = droplevels(data_clean$state)

data_clean$city_or_county[data_clean$city_or_county == ""] = NA
data_clean$city_or_county = droplevels(data_clean$city_or_county)

# Extracted the year and month from the date to be used for more analysis

data_clean$year = substring(data_clean$date, 1, 4)

data_clean$month = substring(data_clean$date, 6, 7)

#changing the data types from characters to numeric

data_clean$year <- as.numeric(data_clean$year)
data_clean$month <- as.numeric(data_clean$month)

EXPOUND UPON THE FOLLOWING: This given dataset was not geared towards predictive modeling, but the variables themselves are interesting and easy to draw insights from. Through the geographic information, we can tell that there are some areas with much more violence than others. Demographic information showed that actors were, typically, in their late teens to mid twenties, and male. Teen actors are more likely to be arrested than killed by police. We did not have any data on the ethnicities of the people who were killed, that could lead to further insights. Summer months are the most active for gun violence. High rates of incidents occurring in January as well. Illinois is the state with the most incidents of gun violence, with Chicago being the most violent city. This insight was made after the sampling of the data, beforehand, Illinois was 3rd, trailing Texas and California Most incidents of gun violence ARE NOT MASS SHOOTINGS! A typical incident doesn’t even have deaths involved. Gun violence is on the rise. 2018’s subtotal shows around 13,000 incidents had occurred, and we only had information for up until March.
